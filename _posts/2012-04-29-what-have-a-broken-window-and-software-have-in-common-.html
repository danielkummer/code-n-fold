The broken window theory

Maybe you've heard of the broken window theory. It was introduced by two social scientists ( James Q. Wilson and George L. Kelling) in an article titled "Broken Windows" which appeared in March 1982.

The title is named after the following example:

"Consider a building with a few broken windows. If the windows are not repaired, the tendency is for vandals to break a few more windows. Eventually, they may even break into the building, and if it's unoccupied, perhaps become squatters or light fires inside. Or consider a sidewalk. Some litter accumulates. Soon, more litter accumulates. Eventually, people even start leaving bags of trash from take-out restaurants there or breaking into cars."

More about this theory can be found on wikipedia.
